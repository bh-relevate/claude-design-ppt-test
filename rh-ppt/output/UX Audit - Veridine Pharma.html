Section 02

Why this matters for pharma

Pharmaceutical web experiences sit at the intersection of regulated content, sensitive audiences, and ADA exposure. Accessibility is not optional.
Patients affected
1 in 4
U.S. adults live with a disability that can affect how they read, hear, or interact with a web page — many of them on therapy.
ADA web lawsuits filed
+8,800
Title III digital accessibility cases filed in U.S. federal court in 2024 — pharma and healthcare among the top five targeted sectors.
ISI compliance risk
Direct
If Important Safety Information cannot be perceived by all users equally, prominence and balance obligations may be considered unmet.

Keyboard users cannot complete core flows

Issue 01 · Critical
ISI drawer trap on mobile
The fixed ISI drawer captures focus when expanded but offers no way to dismiss or return to the page with a keyboard. Screen reader users on iOS are stranded.
#isi-drawer · expanded · no aria-modal · no Esc handler
Issue 02 · Critical
Savings card flow skips a step
The eligibility question on step 2 is rendered as a styled <div> with a click handler. Tab order bypasses it; users complete the form without consenting.
Issue 03 · Serious
Focus indicator is invisible
Default browser outline is suppressed globally with outline: none and no replacement style. Users tabbing through the page have no idea where they are.
Issue 04 · Serious
No "skip to content" link
The site's primary nav contains 11 links. Keyboard and screen-reader users must traverse all of them on every page; a visible-on-focus skip link is missing.

Forms and error messaging

What we observed
  • ·Input labels are visually present but implemented as adjacent text — not programmatically associated with the field.
  • ·Required fields rely on a red asterisk alone; the requirement is never announced to assistive tech.
  • ·Error states color the border red but do not move focus, scroll into view, or describe the error.
  • ·The ISI modal opens with a generic role="dialog" and no accessible name or description.
  • ·Date-of-birth field accepts a single text input — no format hint, no input mask, no parsing tolerance.
Why it matters here
Savings card enrollment is the highest-converting moment on the site. Users who cannot complete the form are users who do not start therapy. This same form gates the patient support program.
Fix priority. A correctly-labelled form with descriptive, focus-receiving error messages would resolve four of the five findings on this slide and is recoverable in a single sprint.

Recommendation

A 90-day path to AA conformance

Three waves of remediation. Wave one ships the critical fixes before the September brand campaign.

Wave 01 · Now

Days 0 — 30
  • CriticalRe-tone CTAs, ISI body, and helper text to pass 4.5 : 1.
  • CriticalReplace ISI drawer with a focus-managed dialog; add Esc + close button.
  • CriticalRefactor savings-card consent step to a real <button>.
  • CriticalRestore a visible 2 px focus ring across all interactive elements.

Wave 02 · Next

Days 30 — 60
  • SeriousAdd a skip-to-content link and fix heading hierarchy in CMS templates.
  • SeriousProgrammatically associate every form label and add descriptive error messages.
  • SeriousAudit and correct ARIA on the audience toggle, accordion, and tabs.
  • SeriousAdd live regions for form submission and ISI expansion events.

Wave 03 · Later

Days 60 — 90
  • ModerateClean up landmark roles and decorative SVG announcements.
  • ModerateDocument the accessible component patterns in the Veridine design system.
  • ProcessAdd automated axe scans to CI; require manual SR pass at QA.
  • ProcessPublish an accessibility statement and feedback channel.
