What the research shows.

Five findings from eleven moderated sessions — the why behind the numbers.

Relevate Health13
Research · Finding 01 of 05Conversational Skills
01

The game builds the conversational skill HCPs need most: identifying why.

Before choosing a response, HCPs need to know which hesitancy they're dealing with.

The concern-identification framework — mapping patient statements to categories like adverse-event fear, public-figure distrust, and social media misinformation — was described as immediately transferable to real patient conversations.

HCPs with lower baseline confidence reported the clearest gains. Those gains were explicitly attributed to the framework, with the statistical data backing it up.

A family medicine physician described going from a confidence score of 4 to 6 specifically for challenging pediatric vaccine conversations — attributing the gain to what she learned in the game's data and dialogue modules.

Research · Finding 03 of 05Receptivity
03

HCPs are receptive — across experience levels and specialties.

Physicians, PAs, family medicine, internal medicine, urgent care, early- and senior-career — the same answer.

Clinicians who grew up on video games described the format as a natural fit. Clinicians who did not described it as a welcome relief from journal articles and compliance modules.

A physician assistant with 10 years in urgent care rated her hesitation at 1 out of 7 and described her experience as: "This is like a real conversation. This is exactly what happens."

Research · Finding 04 of 05Peer Advocacy
04

High recommendation intent becomes a distribution channel.

Among relevant-fit HCPs, recommendation scores averaged above 9 out of 10 — not passive endorsements.

Participants named specific peer contexts where they would share the game: with early-career colleagues, in residency orientations, across multidisciplinary care teams.

An HCP who recommends a learning tool to colleagues is a trusted distribution channel — far more credible than a pharma email or a website banner.
